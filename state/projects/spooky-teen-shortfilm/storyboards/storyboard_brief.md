# Storyboard Brief — Campfire Repeat (3D Woods)

Goal
- Produce rough, readable storyboards to lock vision before 3D styleframes/animation.
- Format: 10 key frames (lo‑fi OK: grayscale/flat color), 16:9 master with center‑safe composition for later vertical crops.
- Tone: Sub–Rated R (PG‑13 intensity), suspense over gore.

Context
- Five teens camping; cell service dies; rule: relighting the campfire snaps time back 5 minutes.
- Persistent variable: phone battery % persists across loops; memories rotate; small prop drift; a chair is eventually missing; a silhouette appears only during Live Photo motion.

Visual Language (targets)
- Moonlit blues vs. campfire ambers; phones as fill light in darkness.
- Readable silhouettes with distinct costumes (hoodie, beanie, flannel, puffer, varsity) and color hints.
- Handheld‑feel framing with occasional lockoffs; shallow DOF for phone inserts.

Deliverables
- 10 PNG frames @ 1920×1080 (OK if you draft at 1280×720); filenames: storyboard_f01.png … storyboard_f10.png
- A contact sheet: storyboard_contact_v0.png (3×4 grid or similar)
- Optional: a JSON/CSV listing captions under each frame (we provide a manifest below).

Save to
- /home/james/Hermes/state/projects/spooky-teen-shortfilm/storyboards/v0/

Acceptance (v0)
- Each frame clearly communicates the beat, lighting mood, and blocking.
- Center‑safe action preserved.
- Battery persistence and the loop rule are visually legible in at least one frame.
- If a user-provided reference image is the stated inspiration, the boards must visibly reflect that reference's subjects, props, composition, and lighting language.
- Abstract circles, boxes, icons, or other schematic placeholders are not review-ready storyboards when a reference-grounded redraw was requested.
- If the lane can only produce blocking diagrams, the output must be labeled placeholder/schematic and reported as blocked for review-ready boards.
