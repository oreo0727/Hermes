# Midnight Signals — Creative Brief

Goal: Develop a 5–7 minute AI‑generated spooky short film aimed at teenagers (13–17). Tone is suspenseful, atmospheric, and phone‑native (vertical or square safe), with clever scares over gore. Theme pillars: friendship, curiosity, digital folklore, and the boundary between online and IRL.

Audience: Teens who binge short‑form horror (YouTube, TikTok), like analog horror vibes, liminal spaces, and urban‑legend twists. Rating target: PG‑13; no graphic violence or lingering cruelty.

Non‑negotiables
- No real‑world doxxing, self‑harm depiction, or exploitative content.
- Consent and safety onscreen: characters make choices, payoffs are earned.
- Clear authorship: we disclose AI use in end card/description.

Format & Delivery
- Runtime: 5–7 minutes.
- Aspect: 16:9 with safe center‑crop for 9:16 and 1:1 derivatives.
- Sound: Headphone‑first mix; avoid infringing music; use original/royalty‑free stems.
- Pipeline (first pass): script → beat‑board → styleframes → animatic (temp VO, SFX) → final renders → grade → captions → deliverables.

Creative North Star
- “Signals in the static.” Phones, school intercoms, gym PA, bus stop boards—all speaking back at night.
- Visual anchors: empty school corridors, flickering gym lights, suburban bus stops, glow of screens, motion‑activated cameras, VHS overlays used sparingly.

Success Criteria
- Teens find it creepy and shareable without being mean‑spirited.
- At least one iconic image + one memorable audio cue.
- Pipeline repeatable for future shorts.
