# 10-Beat Sheet — Campfire Repeat (3D Woods, 5 Teens)

Premise: Every time they relight the campfire, time snaps back five minutes. Small details change, who remembers rotates, and only phone battery percentages persist across loops. Cell service is dead.

Cast: Ava (decisive), Jay (techy), Noor (empathetic), Eli (comic relief), Maya (observer). Antagonist: The loop (agentless) and what it erases if they keep resetting.

1) Cold Open (0:00–0:30)
- Black woods. Embers only. Phone lockscreen glows: 12:05 AM, 3% battery. Title sting. Wind lifts ash… then dies.

2) Setup (0:30–1:00)
- Five teens at a dead fire ring. No bars. Jokes about being offline. They decide to relight for warmth.

3) Rule Discovery (1:00–1:45)
- WHOOSH as flame catches. Quick “time pop” (visual lens warp). A watch reads 12:00 AM again. Ava remembers lighting it already. Others don’t. Ava’s phone: still 3%.

4) Test & Tells (1:45–2:15)
- They mark a tree (stack stones; notch). Douse, relight. Snap. Now Jay remembers; stone stack persists; battery % persist; a mug has moved. Unease.

5) Fun & Dread (2:15–3:00)
- Montage: rapid relight cycles. Props drift, jokes get staler, Noor grows anxious. On loop four, Noor’s text draft is already typed.

6) Midpoint — Missing (3:00–3:45)
- On relight, one camp chair is empty. The group feels “complete,” but Maya freezes—she remembers a sixth laugh on the audio she recorded. Photo reels disagree about who’s present.

7) Escalation (3:45–4:30)
- The treeline holds a shape only visible in Live Photos. They argue: stop relighting vs. one last try to fix the timeline. Wind keeps trying to kick embers alive.

8) Confrontation (4:30–5:20)
- They choose darkness. Scatter the ring, bury coals, unplug comfort. Phones as only light; insects fall silent; the loop strains (flicker on the coals; near-ignition).

9) Choice & Break (5:20–6:10)
- Jay smashes his power bank—constant variable removed. Ava pockets a single coal to keep warm without relighting. The loop… doesn’t snap. A silhouette steps closer—then halts.

10) Button (6:10–7:00)
- Dawn grows. Birds return. Phones chime to life with service—then die again. In Ava’s pocket: a faint glow. Someone behind them clicks a lighter closed. Cut.
